## SUMMARY

All four search targets yielded high-quality, citable sources. For Target 1 (Python pedagogy for business students), I found 8+ peer-reviewed papers (2018–2025) with specific pedagogical findings, including a key result that business students with **no prior programming experience outperform those with some experience** — directly relevant for course design. For Targets 2–3 (neurodivergence statistics + Colombian legal framework), official WHO, CDC, BLS, and Colombian academic sources provide verified statistics spanning global to local levels. For Target 4 (service quality/KPI frameworks), the CASP, BHCOE, Silbaugh (2022), and Townsend (2024) papers define the exact kind of QD-KPI framework you need.

| Field | Data |

| **Year** | 2021 (Aug) |
| **DOI/ERIC** | ERIC: EJ1310052 |

### What could not be found:
5. **Colombia national university disability statistics** — No centralized public database found. DANE 2018 census has disability data but not disaggregated by diagnosis (TDAH/TEA). Individual university reports would need direct institutional contact.
6. **Cost of therapy sessions in Colombia specifically** — No published price schedule found. U.S. FAIR Health data (Autism Speaks) available as proxy. Colombian insurance data (EPS tariffs under Ley 1616) might be accessed through Ministerio de Salud SISPRO database.

10. **Colombia TEA national prevalence (official)** — No Ministerio de Salud national prevalence survey for TEA was found publicly accessible. The Bogotá school data (García-Zambrano 2025) and the 51.1/10,000 figure (Beltrán Dulcey 2016) are the best available Colombian estimates.
